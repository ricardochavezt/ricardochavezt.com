  @imstillculture on Instagram: "Charles Schulz introduced Snoopy on October 4, 1950, just two days after the Peanuts comic strip debuted. In these early drawings, the iconic beagle looked very different from his modern version because he walked on all four legs and acted like a normal puppy. Schulz originally wanted to name the character Sniffy, but he changed it after remembering his mother’s suggestion that their next family dog should be named Snoopy. 

  The cartoonist based many of the character’s traits on his clever childhood dog named Spike, who famously ate unusual items like pins and tacks. Over the next decade, the silent pet evolved significantly as he began walking upright, sleeping on top of his doghouse, and sharing his imaginative inner thoughts with the world."
